European Museums I Visited in 2018

Pablo Picasso Museum

Barcelona

Admission: €12

Although more famous for its Guadi works (Sagrada Familia, Parc Guell), Barcelona is also home to one of the most extensive collections of Picasso works. Featuring 4,251 works by the artist, the Museu Picasso guides visitors through the first works of his career to some of his most ambitious endeavors.

Highlights of the Museu Picasso include:

  • Science and Charity
  • Las Meninas
  • Los pichones

Bode Museum

Berlin

Admission: €12

The Bode Museum is a relatively small museum on Museum Island in Berlin featuring a collection of sculptures, Byzantine art, coins, and metals. The sculpture collection showcases art from the Middle Ages, Italian Gothic, and early Renaissance periods.

Highlights from the museum include:

  • Antonio Canova, Dancer
  • Donatello, Pazzi Madona
  • Pietro Bernini, Satyr with Panther
  • Raffael, The Virgin and Blessing Child with the Infant t. John

Lourve Museum

Paris

Admission: €15-€17

It doesn't get any bigger than the Lourve. From the iconic building, to the madness of La Jaconde (also known as the "Mona Lisa"), this Parisian art instituion is a must when visiting the City of Lights. You'll want to spend a significant chunk of time visiting The Lourve as it contains a huge collection of sculptures, paintings, and antiquities.

Highlights from the museum include:

  • Leonardo Da Vinci, La Jaconde (the "Mona Lisa")
  • Venus de Milo
  • Large Sphinx
  • The Winged Victory of Samothrace
  • Eugène Delacroix, Liberty Leading the People

Museum d'Orsay

Paris

Admission: €14

Across the Seine from the Lourve Museum is another Parisian art gem, the Musée d'Orsay. My personal favorite of the European art museums, the d'Orsay features the largest collection of Impressionist and post-Impressionist masterpieces in the world, including works by Monet, Manet, Gaugin, Renoir, Degas, and Van Gogh among others.

Highlights of the museum include:

  • Manet, The Luncheon on the Grass
  • Renoir, Bal au moulin de la Galette, Montmarte
  • Monet, Series of The Poplars
  • Van Gogh, Starry Night Over the Rhone
  • Degas, The Blue Dancers

Palace of Versailles

Versailles

Admission: €20-€27

The sumptuous seat of the French monarchy, the Versailles Palace is the second most-visited monument in the Île-de-France region (behind the Lourve museum and ahead of the Eiffel Tower). I recommend either getting the audioguide or joining a tour so that you make the most of your time in the Palace.

Highlights of the palace include:

  • The Hall of Mirrors
  • The sculpture gardens

Bonus: Berlin East Side Gallery

Berlin

Admission: free

In homage to Berlin's totalitarian history (both under the Third Reich and then subsequently under Communist control), the Berlin East Side Gallery features 105 paintings from artists around the world representing peace, unity, free expression, and a better future for people all over the world. At the time of commission, the political and social division between West and East Germany was ending and the works represent a time of great euphoria for Berliners.

Highlights from the gallery include:

  • Dimitri Vrubel, My God, Help Me to Survive This Deadly Love
  • Thierry Noir, untitled
  • Salvadore de Fazio, Dawn of Peace
